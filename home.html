1. PRE-SALES

Customers can have a lot of really valuable insights on how our product is used and how it can be improved. It's important to acknowledge this, especially if it's a feature we would like to implement.

My response: Hi _______, We really appreciate you taking the time to share your thoughts with us. It's important to us to know what the community is interested in. [FEATURE] isn't something we currently offer, however, we do have some other exciting features on our plate that you might find to be useful, like [X, Y, Z].

I would offer solutions that are similar to what they are looking for, and that are unique to our brand. If the feature is something in the works, I would make that clear in the response.

2. FIRE ALARM

Customer Support Specialists are on the front lines, and often bear the brunt of distraught or unhappy customers. So how we handle these moments are crucial.

Entering this kind of scenario, the first thing to do would be to troubleshoot where the issue is coming from and what's causing it to happen. With a better understanding of the issue, I can then decide if it's within my realm of capabilities to resolve or if I need to reach out to the appropriate department or supervisor. Next would be to face the customers! This is an important part that has to be handled perfectly. I would apologize sincerely, sympathize and accept responsibility. As an ambassador for the company, a customer service team has to take responsibility for a customer's unhappiness, whether it is their fault or not. Finally, I would inform them of the steps we are taking to fix the problem.
